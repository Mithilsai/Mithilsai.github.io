Chronicles of Andhra Pradesh

An exhaustive, professionally curated historical archive based on a 400-point timeline. Every ruler, inscription, title, feudatory, and societal milestone has been documented in chronological sequence.

200 BCE – 200 CE

Satavahanas: Genesis & Early Kings

The first kingdom to rule the Andhra region, presiding for nearly 400 to 500 years. Their official language was Prakrit.

  • Origin Theories:
    • Andhra: P.V. Parabrahma Sastry, Maramanda Ramarao, Gutti Venkatrao, Burgess, Barnett, Gopalachari, V.A. Smith, E.J. Rapson, R.G. Bhandarkar.
    • Maharashtra: P.T. Srinivasa Iyengar, Joglekar, H.C. Raychaudhuri.
    • Vidarbha: V.V. Mirashi.
    • Karnataka: Sukthankar.
  • Literary Evidence: Mentioned in Bhagavata, Matsya, Vayu, Vishnu, and Brahmanda Puranas. Matsya Purana states 30 kings ruled for 450 years. Vishnu Purana notes 17 kings. Ashoka's 13th Rock Edict mentions the Andhras.
  • Capital Shift Theories:
    • Burgess & Barnett: Srikakulam (Krishna) → Dhanyakatakam/Dharanikota (Amaravati) → Pratishthanapuram (Paithan).
    • Mirashi, Raychaudhuri, Sastry: Pratishthanapuram → Dhanyakatakam.
    • Gunadhya (Brihatkatha Manjari): Pratishthanapuram.
  • Foundations: Based on inscriptions/coins/Somadeva's Kathasaritasagara, the Mula Purusha (Original Ancestor) is Satavahana (Sadavahana). Per Puranas, the Empire Founder & First King is Srimukha.
  • Srimukha: Ruled 23 years from Pratishthanapuram. Feudatories of Mauryas. Ashoka gave him the title "Raya" and referred to the dynasty as "Satyaputras". Followed Jainism, then Vedic/Brahminism.
  • Krishna (Kanha): Brother of Srimukha. Took the throne because Srimukha's son was a minor. Bhagavata Religion (Krishna worship) entered Deccan during his rule.
  • Satakarni I: Son of Srimukha. Contemporaries: Pushyamitra Sunga (Magadha) and Kharavela (Chedi/Kalinga). Defeated Sunga and issued Ujjain symbol coins. Defeated by Kharavela (recorded in Hathigumpha Inscription). Performed 2 Ashvamedha & 1 Rajasuya yagas. First Indian king to donate land to Brahmins (13 villages, 36,000 silver coins, 44,000 cows, 1,000 horses, 10 elephants).
    • Wife: Naganika (Daughter of Tranakariyo, King of Maharathi Ambi). She issued the Naneghat Inscription in Prakrit.
    • Titles: Ekaveera, Dakshinapathapati, Sura, Apratihatachakra (Naneghat); Samrat, Mahan (Puranas); Mallakarna, Gohiranyabhudana.
  • Purnotsunga (Vedasiri): During his rule, Kharavela attacked Bhattiprolu. Next minor king: Skandastambhi.
Middle Satavahana Era

Cultural Summits & Language Shifts

  • Satakarni II: Ruled 56 years. Conquered Magadha and Kalinga (Yuga Purana & Gargi Samhita note he ruled Vidisha for 10 years). Built the Dakshina Torana (South Gateway) to Ashoka's Sanchi Stupa. His court artist, Ananda, issued the Sanchi inscription praising him.
  • Minor Kings: Apilaka, Meghaswati.
  • Kuntala Satakarni: Defeated Shakas in 58 BCE, earning the title Vikramaditya and starting the Vikrama Era. Changed official language from Prakrit to Sanskrit. Mentioned in Kavya Mimamsa (Rajasekhara), Brihatkatha (Gunadhya), and Kamasutra (Vatsyayana notes his wife Malayavati died during the 'Kari Rata' sexual act).
    • Court Poets: Sarvavarma (Sanskrit, wrote Katantra Vyakaranam in 6 months to teach the king, made head of Bharukachcha/Broach). Gunadhya (Prakrit, wrote Brihatkatha, which was later destroyed).
  • Pulamavi I: Destroyed the Kanva dynasty by killing King Susharma.
  • Hala (17th King): The first "Kaviraja" in South India. Title: Kavivatsala. Married Sri Lankan princess Lilavati at Sapta Godavari (story recorded in Lilavati Parinayam by Kutuhala). His rule was the Golden Age of Prakrit. He authored Gatha Saptasati (700 stories, beginning with a Shiva Stotram).
Late Satavahana Era

Gautamiputra & The Final Epoch

  • Gautamiputra Satakarni (23rd King): Greatest ruler. Wife: Vashishti. Started metronymic tradition (mother's name first). Started Salivahana Era in 78 CE. Fought the Battle of Jogalthambi, killing Shaka ruler Nahapana (Kshaharata dynasty) and restruck his silver coins. Followed Vedic religion, encouraged Buddhism.
    • Mother: Gautami Balasri issued the Nasik Prasasti.
    • Titles: Trisamudratoyapitavahana, Dakshinasamudradhiswara, Shakayavanapahlavanisudhana, Dwijakulavardhana, Ekabrahmana, Agamanilaya, Kshatriyadarpanamanamardana, Varnasankaryanirodhaka, Ekadhanurdhara, Ekasura, Benakatakaswami, Kshaharata Vamsa Niravaseshakara.
  • Pulamavi II (Vashishtiputra): Titles: Dakshinapatheswara, Navanagara Swami. Shifted capital to Amaravati. Defeated by Shaka King Rudradaman (recorded in the Junagadh/Girnar Inscription—India's 1st Sanskrit/Brahmi inscription).
  • Vashishtiputra Sivasri Satakarni: Married Rudradaman's daughter. Title: Kshatrapa.
  • Yajna Sri Satakarni: Last great ruler. Commander: Bhavagopa. Issued Chinnaganjam inscription. Banabhatta's Harshacharita calls him Trisamudradhiswara. Matsya Purana compiled during his rule.
    • Patronized Acharya Nagarjuna (born in Vedali, Indian Einstein / 2nd Buddha/Tathagata / Indian Martin Luther). Nagarjuna founded Mahayana Buddhism, wrote Suhru Lekhanam (letters to Yajna Sri), Prajnaparamita Sastra, Mula Madhyamaka Sastra, Sunya Saptasati, Lalitavistara, Karandavyuha, Sabhavativyuha. (Note: Samayasaram was by Acharya Kundakunda, Chittashuddi Prakaranam by Aryadeva).
    • Yajna built the Mahavihara (1500 rooms, 7 floors) at Sriparvata (Nagarjunakonda) for Nagarjuna. Somadeva's Kathasaritasagaram claims Yajna Sri's son killed Nagarjuna.
  • Vijaya Sri Satakarni: Built Vijayapuri near Sriparvata.
  • Chanda Sri Satakarni: Issued Kodavali inscription.
  • Pulamavi III / IV: Last ruler. Issued Myakadoni, Holehasan, and Adoni inscriptions. Overthrown by his feudatory Sri Chantamula (Ikshvaku) and fled to Bellary.
Satavahana Society

Admin, Economy & Guilds

  • Administration: Kingdom → Aharas (headed by Amatyas) → Nagaras (headed by Nigama Sabha: 30 members, 6 committees of 5) → Gramas (headed by Gramini/Gulmika).
  • Officials: Mahakaryaka (village dispute solver), Rajukas (judges/judicial), Rajjugahakudu (land surveyor), Gahapatis (caste heads).
  • Economy: Land tax was 1/6th. Tax on artisans: Kurukara. Produced "Wootz" steel (evidence in Kondapur, Medak). Foreign traders: Sardhavahulu.
    • Tools: Udaga yantram (ploughing), Ghati yantram (water lifting), Garika yantram (cotton ginning—invented by Rakkasi Lothai under Pulamavi II).
    • Coins: Lead (highest volume), Potin (alloy), Silver (Karshapanas, silver+copper), Gold (Suvarna). 1 Suvarna = 35 Karshapanas.
  • Guilds (Srenis): Headed by Setti/Sreshti. Acted as banks (12% interest). Notable guilds: Kolikalu (weavers), Tilapishtakulu (oil pressers), Kasakarulu (bronze smiths), Kularulu (potters), Tesakarulu (polishers), Kamarulu (blacksmiths), Halikulu (farmers), Veja (doctors), Udayantrikulu (machine makers), Kanchukulu (palace guards), Kubjalu (elephant caretakers), Pratiharalu (door keepers), Chankukarulu (leather), Gadhikulu (perfumes), Suvarnakarulu (goldsmiths), Vadakulu (carpenters), Selavadakulu (sculptors), Pasakarulu (bamboo/basket), Dhanyakulu (grain merchants).
  • Culture: Oldest Buddhist stupa in AP: Bhattiprolu. Dhanyakatakam known as Purvasailam in Buddhist literature. Educational institutes: Ghatikas.
220 CE – 300 CE

The Ikshvakus (Sri Parvatiya Andhras)

Mentioned in Bhavishya Purana. Capital: Vijayapuri. Emblem: Lion. Divided kingdom into Rashtras (headed by Mahatalavaras). 5 villages = Grama Panchaka. Started 'Veeragal' (suicide guards) and grandfather-naming traditions.

  • Origin Theories: North India (Rapson, Buhler), Karnataka (Vogel, Sten Konow), Tamil Nadu (Gopalachari), Coastal Krishna/Andhra (Caldwell).
  • Sri Chantamula (Vasishthiputra Chantamula): Founder/First King. Defeated Pulamavi IV. Devotee of Kartikeya (Virupaksha). Performed Ashvamedha, Agnistoma, Agnihotra, Vajapeya. Issued Rentala, Dachepalli, Kesanapalli inscriptions. Titles: Maharaja, Sata Sahasra Halaka, Mahadanadhipati, Apratihatasankapana.
  • Virapurushadatta (Son): Fought no wars. Only king to embrace Buddhism ("Golden Age of Buddhism in AP"). Called "South Indian Ashoka". Built India's 1st Buddhist University (Sri Parvata University). Issued Alluri Inscription.
  • Ehuvala Chantamula (Chantamula II): First to build Hindu temples in South India (Pushpabhadra Narayana, Kartikeya, Nodigiswara/Nandikeswara, Devi temples at Nagarjunakonda). His commander Elisiri built Kumaraswami temple (Yeleswaram). Issued Nagarjunakonda Inscription (First Sanskrit Inscription in South India).
  • Rudrapurushadatta (Virapurushadatta II): Last ruler. Built Pushpabhadra Narayana temple. Defeated by Pallava King Simhavarma, ending the dynasty (Manchikallu Inscription).
  • Other Notes: Sivasena (commander of Abhira king Sakasena) built Ashtabhuja Narayana Swamy Temple (1st Vaishnava temple in AP). Harathi temple visited for fertility. Bhavaviveka wrote Tarkajwala, Karataratna, Prajnapradeepa. Guild leaders: Ulikapramukha (handloom), Pusina Sreni (sweets), Parnika Sreni (betel leaves).
300 CE – 624 CE

Salankayanas, Ananda Gotras & Vishnukundins

  • Salankayanas: Capital: Vengi (Pedavegi). Mula Purusha: Vijayadevavarma. Founder: Hastivarma (defeated by Samudragupta; Allahabad Pillar calls him Vaingeyaka). Family deity: Chitrarathaswami (Sun). Issued Pedavegi & Guntupalli inscriptions. King order: Hastivarma → Nandivarma I / Devavarma → Achandavarma → Vijayanandivarma / Nandivarma II. Conquered Ananda Gotras. Ended by Vishnukundins.
  • Ananda Gotras: Capital: Kandarapuram (Kandara rulers). Emblem: Vrushabham/Nandi. Victory banner: Golungula (Monkey). Defeated by Salankayanas.
  • Vishnukundins: Language: Sanskrit. Emblem: Lion (Kesari). Divided kingdom into Vishayas. Land measured in Nivartanas. Built cave temples (Mogalrajapuram, Undavalli - featuring Anantha Padmanabha Swamy).
    • Indravarma: Founder. Title: Priyaputra. Issued Ramatirtham inscription.
    • King order: Indravarma → Madhavavarma I → Govindavarma → Madhavavarma II → Vikramendravarma I → Vikramendravarma II.
    • Madhavavarma II (Greatest): Defeated Salankayanas/Anandas. Shifted capital: Amarapuram → Indrapalanagaram → Denduluru (Vengi). Titles: Trivaranagara Bhavana Sundari, Janasraya, Trimudradhipati.
    • Inscriptions: Tummalagudem, Chaitanyapuri, Khanapur.
    • Linguistic Milestones: First Telugu word 'Nagabu' (Amaravati inscription). First Telugu sentence 'Vijaya Rajya Samvatsarambul' (Chikkulla copper plate).
624 CE – 9th Century

Eastern Chalukyas: Genesis

Western Chalukya King Pulakesin II defeated Vishnukundins and gave Vengi to his brother Kubja Vishnuvardhana. Admin: Rashtras → Vishayas → Gramas. Tax: 1/6 (collected by Gramakutudu). Emblem: Varaham. Language: Sanskrit. Capitals: Pistapuram (Pithapuram) → Vengi → Rajahmundry.

  • Kubja Vishnuvardhana (Founder): Titles: Vishamasiddhi, Maharaja (Timmapuram inscr), Makaradhwaja, Kamadeva. Issued Chezerla inscription (>50% Telugu poems). Built Kunti Madhava temple (Pithapuram). Wife Ayyana Mahadevi built Nedumbasadi (Jain rest house in Vijayawada for Kavururi Jains); Kubja donated Mushinikonda village to it. Chinese pilgrim Hiuen Tsang visited Dhanyakatakam for 2 yrs to study Tripitakas from Subhuti & Surya.
  • Jayasimha I (Jayasimha Vallabha): Titles: Sarvalokasraya, Sarvasiddhi, Prithvi Vallabha. Shifted capital to Vengi. Founded Sarvasiddhi town in Vizag. Issued Vipparla Inscription. Defeated Boya kings (Pedamaddali Inscr).
  • Indra Bhattaraka: Ruled 7 days. Title: Tyagathenu. Issued Kondanaguru inscr.
  • Vishnuvardhana II: Title: Pralayaditya. Issued Reyuru inscr.
  • Mangi Yuvaraja: Title: Samastabhuvanasraya.
  • Jayasimha II: Title: Niravadya.
  • Vishnuvardhana III: Restored Nedumbasadi & Mushinikonda. Vengi-Pallava conflict began.
  • Vijayaditya I: Dantidurga overthrew Badami Chalukyas to form Rashtrakutas. Vengi-Rashtrakuta feud began.
  • Vishnuvardhana IV: Defeated by Rashtrakuta Dhruva I (mentioned in Pampa's Vikramarjuna Vijayam).
9th – 10th Century CE

Eastern Chalukyas: The Golden Age

  • Vijayaditya II: Overthrown by Rashtrakuta Govinda III & Bhima Saluki. Fled to Bezawada (named Vijayawada after him). Returned after 12 years. Fought 108 battles, built 108 Narendra Mrigeswara temples. Title: Narendra Mrigaraja. Victories noted in Sataluru inscription (issued by his grandson).
  • Gunaga Vijayaditya III (Greatest): Real name Vijayaditya III. Titles: Gunakenalla, Ranaranga Sudraka, Tripuramartya Maheswara, Parachakrarama, Bhuvanakandarpa, Dakshinapathapati, Prithvi Vallabha, Samadhigata Panchamahashabda. Defeated by Amoghavarsha (Battle of Vengavalli) and became vassal. Later defeated Krishna II, capturing Kiranapura, Achalapura, Tripurapura. Issued Sataluru inscr.
    • Commanders: Kadiyaraju, Vinayadi Sharma, Rajaditya, and Panduranga (Chief).
    • Panduranga captured Boya Kottas (Nellore), built Pandurangapuram at Pulicat, built Panduranga Maheswaralayam, and issued the Addanki Inscription (1st Telugu poetic inscr).
  • Chalukya Bhima I (Vishnuvardhana VI): Captured by Vemulawada king Baddena ("like a crocodile in water" - Pampa's Vikramarka Vijayam). Rescued by Mudigonda Chalukya Kusumadhvaja (Title: Vengi Bandhavimukti - Korivi inscr). Battle of Niravadyapura (Nidadavole): his son Iramartiganda & Rashtrakuta Gundaya both died. Built Pancharamas (Kumarabhemeswara, Bhimeswara, Someswara, Ksheeraramalingeswara, Amareswara). Fought 360 battles (Pithapuram inscr by Mallapadeva). Founded Chalukya Bhimavaram. Donated Attili to singer Challavva (Title: Gandharva Vidya Visarada). Court Poet: Vamana/Bhatta Vamana (wrote Kavyalankara Sutra, title Kavivrushabha). Commander Chattappa built Pardheswara temple on Indrakeeladri hill.
  • Vijayaditya IV: Title: Kollabiganda. Fought Viraja battle against Kalinga Vajrahasta I.
  • Amma Raja I: Alt theory says he shifted capital to Rajahmundry.
  • Kanthakavi Vijayaditya: Ruled 15 days.
  • Bhima Raja: Ruled 8 months of anarchy (Maliyampudi inscr).
  • Yuddhamalla II: Built Kartikeyalayam in Vijayawada. Issued Bezawada inscr. Feudatory Nannechoda translated Kumarasambhavam into Telugu.
  • Chalukya Bhima II: Followed by Amma Raja II (son of Lokambika) and Danarnava (son of Urdhava).
10th – 12th Century CE

Eastern Chalukyas: Decline & Chola Merger

  • Amma Raja II: Titles: Bhattadeva, Madhava Bhattu, Parama Brahmana. Addakavi (Jain) built viharas. Wife Chamekamba requested Kaluchumburru donation to Jains. Kalamukha Shaivism entered. Issued Maliyampudi, Kaluchumburru inscriptions.
  • Danarnava: Killed Amma II with help of Kakatiya Gundana (Korivi). Issued Magallu Inscr. Title: Rajanarayana. Built Mandavya Narayana (Samalkot). Killed by Jata Choda Bhima. (Note: Tailapa II killed Rashtrakuta Kokkaraja II to form Kalyani Chalukyas).
  • Jata Choda Bhima (Telugu Choda): Amma II's brother-in-law. Title: Choda Trinetrudu (killed Vaidumba king Bhuvana Trinetra). Defeated by Rajaraja Chola. Kailasanatha inscr details his takeover.
  • Saktivarma: Crowned by Rajaraja Chola. Title: Chalukya Chandra (coins found in Burma). Vengi became a Chola-Kalyani battlefield. Issued Prabuparru inscr.
  • Vimaladitya: Titles: Rajamartanda, Mummadi Bhima. Shaiva -> Jain (influenced by Trikalayogi Siddhanti, per Ranastapudi inscr). Built Ramakonda cave. Married Kundavva (Chola) & Melamma (Telugu Choda).
  • Rajaraja Narendra: Crowned via Rajendra Chola (married Ammangidevi). Defeated Vijayaditya VII (Battle of Kalidindi, built 3 Shiva temples). Made vassal by Kalyani Chalukya Someswara, who appointed Narayana Bhattu as representative. Founded Rajahmundry (per Kavyalankara Sutra), built Vishnu temple there. Title: Kavyagita Pritiparudu. Donated Nandampudi to Narayana Bhattu.
    • Nannaya: Adikavi, Vagasasanudu, Vidyadayityudu. Wrote Andhra Shabda Chintamani/Andhra Bhashanusasanam in Sanskrit. Wrote Indra Vijayam, Chamundeswari Vilasam in Telugu. Translated 2.5 parvas of Mahabharata (Jaya Samhita) with Narayana Bhattu's help (Titles: Ashtakavisekhara, Kareebhavajrankudu). Tikkana did 4-18; Yerrapragada finished Aranya (Kavitrayam).
    • Pavuluri Mallanna: Titles: Sukavi, Satkalishudu. Translated Mahavirachari's Ganitasara Sangraham (1st Telugu science book). Received Navakhandavada.
  • Rajendra II (Kulottunga Chola I): Son of Rajaraja. Founded Kulottunga Cholapuram (Visakhapatnam). Jayagondar wrote Kalinga Parani about his naval victory. Handed Vengi to his uncle Vijayaditya VII (Last Vengi Ruler).
11th – 12th Century CE

Vengi Feudatories (Velanati, Haihayas, Konidena)

  • Velanati Cholas/Chodas: Capital: Chandole. Called Andhra "Swabhagini Vishayam". Mula purusha: Gonkaraja I. Independence: Gonkaraja II. Battle of Draksharama: Yuvaraja Rajendra II killed early Kakatiya Prolaraja II. Poet Manchena wrote Keyurabahu Charitam in Rajendra II's court. Last king Prithviswara killed by Kakatiya Ganapatideva (Ganapati got title: Prithviswara Sira Krida Vinodakara).
  • Kona Haihayas: Capital: Rakuduru. Founder: Kirtiraja. First in AP history to introduce Dual Kingship (Mummadi Bhima II & Satyaraja).
  • Palnati Haihayas: Capital: Gurazala. Founder: Betaraja (Titles: Haihaya Rajya Pratishtakara, Sanivara Siddhi). Anuguraja's sons: Nalagama (married Mailamma, daughter of Velanati Rajendra II) & Malideva. Ministers: Nagamma (Shaiva, built Virabhadra at Gurazala) & Brahma Naidu (Veera Vaishnava, built Chennakeshava at Macherla). Veera Vaishnavism started by Brahma Naidu (Veera Shaivism by Basaveshwara).
  • The Palnadu War (1176-1182 CE): Fought between Nalagama (supported by Kakatiya Prataparudra I & Nagamma) and Malideva (supported by Brahma Naidu). Began after a cockfight exile. Nalagama won. Recorded by Srinatha (Palnati Veera Charitra) and Mudugu Virabhadra Kavi (Palnati Virabhagavatam).
  • Konidena Cholas: King Nannechoda was the 1st "Kaviraja" in Telugu (translated Kalidasa's Kumarasambhavam into Telugu).
995 CE – 1157 CE

The Early Kakatiyas (Vassals)

Originally from the Durjaya Clan (per Bayyaram Inscr). Emblem: Varaham. Capitals: Hanumakonda → Orugallu. Language: Sanskrit. Bayyaram Inscription notes founder as Venna Bhupati.

  • Kakatiya Gundana: The root founder (Mula Purusha), verified by Danarnava's Magallu Inscription (helped Danarnava defeat Amma Raja II).
  • Beta Raja I: Son of Gundana. Titles: Samanta Vishti Vamsa Kakati Puradhinatha, Chodakshmapala, Chalamuvardha Pramadana. Issued Sanigaram Inscription (written by minister Naranayya, who renovated Yuddhamalla Jain temple at Sanigaram).
  • Prola Raja I: Titles: Arigajakesari, Kakativallabha, Samadhigata Panchamahashabda. Built Kesamudram and Jagatakesarisamudram lakes.
  • Beta Raja II: Titles: Vikramachakra, Chalamartiganda, Tribhuvanamalla, Mahamandaleswara. Issued Kazipet Inscription. Minister: Vaija Dandanadhudu (great statesman). Built Beteswara Shiva temple at Sivapuram. Initiated into Kalamukha Shaivism by Rameswara Pandita.
  • Durga Raja: Donated Beteswara temple to Rameswara Pandita (detailed in Kazipet Inscription).
  • Prola Raja II: Greatest of the early Kakatiyas. Planted victory pillar at Srisailam. Title: Daridraya Bhadravana (for agricultural efforts). Built Swayambhu temple at Orugallu. Minister Betanamatya was Jain; his wife Mailama built Kadalalalaya Basadi in Hanumakonda. Killed in the Battle of Draksharama by Velanati Rajendra II.
1158 CE – 1323 CE

The Imperial (Independent) Kakatiyas

  • Rudradeva (Prataparudra I / Kakatiya Rudra): Declared independence in 1162 CE (Hanumakonda Inscription, written by Achitendra). Issued Ganapavaram inscription. Titles: Vinayabhushana/Vidyabhushana (Draksharama inscr), Kavi Bhasha Poshaka (Pillalamarri inscr), Dayagajakesari.
    • Authored Nitisaram in Sanskrit.
    • Built Rudrasamudra lake and the 1000 Pillar Temple (Rudreshwaralayam) in Hanumakonda. Founded Orugallu and partially shifted capital.
    • Minister Gangadhara (Vaishnava) built Prasanna Kesavalayam.
    • Commander Kota Senani captured Dharanikota (Title: Kota Gelpata).
    • Supported Nalagama in the Palnadu War.
    • Killed by Yadava King Jaitugi/Jaitrapala of Devagiri (recorded by court poet Hemadri in Chaturvarga Sara Grandham).
  • Mahadeva: Brother of Rudradeva. Wife: Bayyambika. Children: Ganapatideva, Mailamba (issued Bayyaram Inscription), Kundamamba. Both daughters married Satavadi king Rudraraja. Guru: Dhruveswara (Shaiva). Killed by Jaitugi while avenging his brother. His son Ganapatideva was taken hostage.
  • Ganapatideva: Released by Jaitugi's son Singhana (Singhana's Titles: Telungaraya Sira Kamatotpatana, Telungaraya Sthapanacharya) to act as a buffer state against Hoysalas and Kalachuris. Ruled 63 years (1199-1262), the longest in AP history. Defeated Velanati Chola Prithviswara.
    • Commander: Recharla Rudra saved the kingdom from crisis during Ganapati's capture. (Titles: Kakatiya Rajya Bhara Dhaureya, Kakatiya Rajya Samarthudu).
    • Ganapati's Titles: Andhradhishudu, Prithviswara, Krida Vinoda, Chodakataka Churakara, Sakaladesa Pratishtapanacharya (Mandena Inscr).
