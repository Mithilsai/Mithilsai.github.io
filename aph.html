200 BCE – 200 CE

Satavahanas: Genesis & Early Kings

TL;DR Primer

  • First unified Deccan empire (ruled 400-500 yrs); Official language was Prakrit.
  • Founded by Mula Purusha Satavahana & Empire King Srimukha.
  • Satakarni I performed 2 Ashvamedha Yagas, and was the first in India to donate land to Brahmins.
  • Origin Theories: Andhra: P.V. Parabrahma Sastry, Maramanda Ramarao, Gutti Venkatrao, Burgess, Barnett, Gopalachari, V.A. Smith, E.J. Rapson, R.G. Bhandarkar. Maharashtra: P.T. Srinivasa Iyengar, Joglekar, H.C. Raychaudhuri. Vidarbha: V.V. Mirashi. Karnataka: Sukthankar.
  • Literary Evidence: Bhagavata, Matsya (mentions 30 kings ruled for 450 yrs), Vayu, Vishnu (mentions 17 kings), and Brahmanda Puranas. Mentioned as "Andhras" in Ashoka's 13th Rock Edict.
  • Capital Shift Theories: 1. Srikakulam → Dhanyakatakam (Amaravati) → Pratishthanapuram (Paithan) [Burgess & Barnett]. 2. Pratishthanapuram → Dhanyakatakam[V.V. Mirashi, Raychaudhuri, Srinivasa Sastry]. 3. Pratishthanapuram [Gunadhya].
Roots
Mula Purusha: Satavahana (Sadavahana) (based on coins, inscriptions, and Somadeva's Kathasaritasagara). Empire Founder/1st King: Srimukha (per Puranas).
Srimukha
Ruled 23 yrs (Matsya Purana). Former Mauryan feudatory. Ashoka gave him title "Raya" and called his lineage "Satyaputras". Religion: Jainism → Vedic.
Krishna (Kanha)
Brother of Srimukha. Took throne as Srimukha's son was minor. Bhagavata Religion (Krishna) entered Deccan during his rule.
Battles
Contemporaries: Pushyamitra Sunga & Kharavela. Defeated Sunga (issued Ujjain symbol coins). Defeated by Kharavela (recorded in Hathigumpha Inscr - though Indian history credits Kharavela, AP history credits Satakarni).
Naneghat
Wife Naganika (Princess of Maharathi Ambi, daughter of Tranakariyo) issued Naneghat Inscription in Prakrit detailing his victories.
Religion
Performed 2 Ashvamedha, 1 Rajasuya. First in India to donate land to Brahmins (exactly 13 villages, 36,001 silver coins, 44,000 cows, 1,000 horses, 10 elephants).
Titles
Ekaveera, Dakshinapathapati, Sura, Apratihatachakra (Naneghat); Samrat, Mahan (Puranas); Mallakarna, Gohiranyabhudana.
Purnotsunga
(Also known as Vedasiri). Kharavela attacked Bhattiprolu during his rule.
Minor
Followed by Skandastambhi.
Middle Satavahana Era

Cultural Summits & Language Shifts

TL;DR Primer

  • Satakarni II conquered Magadha, built Sanchi's South Gateway.
  • Kuntala Satakarni shifted official language to Sanskrit.
  • Hala initiated the Golden Age of Prakrit literature.
Conquests
Ruled 56 years. Conquered Magadha and Kalinga (Yuga Purana & Gargi Samhita state he defeated Sungas and ruled Vidisha for 10 yrs). Followed by minor kings Apilaka and Meghaswati.
Architecture
Built the Dakshina Torana (South Gateway) to Ashoka's Sanchi Stupa.
Inscription
Court artist Ananda issued the Sanchi Inscription praising him.
Literature
Changed official language from Prakrit to Sanskrit. Mentioned in Rajasekhara's Kavya Mimamsa, Gunadhya's Brihatkatha, and Vatsyayana's Kamasutra (wife Malayavati died during dangerous act 'Kari Rata').
Poets
Sarvavarma (Sanskrit, wrote Katantra Vyakaranam in 6 months to teach king, appointed head of Bharukachcha/Broach). Gunadhya (Prakrit, wrote Brihatkatha in the forest, which king ordered destroyed).
Battles
Defeated Shakas (58 BCE), earning title Vikramaditya and starting Vikrama Era.
Pulamavi I
Destroyed the Kanva dynasty by killing King Susharma.
Hala
First "Kaviraja" in South India. Title: Kavivatsala. Married Sri Lankan princess Lilavati at Sapta Godavari (recorded in Lilavati Parinayam by Kutuhala). Golden Age of Prakrit. Authored Gatha Saptasati (700 stories, begins with Shiva Stotram).
Late Satavahana Era

Gautamiputra & The Final Epoch

TL;DR Primer

  • Gautamiputra revived the empire, killed Shaka Nahapana.
  • Yajna Sri patronized Acharya Nagarjuna (Mahayana Buddhism).
  • Empire ended under Pulamavi IV due to Ikshvaku revolt.
Facts
Greatest ruler. Wife: Vashishti. Started metronymic tradition. Started Salivahana Era (78 CE).
Battles
Battle of Jogalthambi: Killed Shaka ruler Nahapana (Kshaharata dynasty) and restruck his silver coins.
Inscription
Mother Gautami Balasri issued the Nasik Prasasti.
Titles
Trisamudratoyapitavahana, Dakshinasamudradhiswara, Shakayavanapahlavanisudhana, Dwijakulavardhana, Ekabrahmana, Agamanilaya, Kshatriyadarpanamanamardana, Varnasankaryanirodhaka, Ekadhanurdhara, Ekasura, Benakatakaswami, Kshaharata Vamsa Niravaseshakara. Followed Vedic religion, encouraged Buddhism.
Pulamavi II
(Vashishtiputra). Titles: Dakshinapatheswara, Navanagara Swami. Shifted capital to Amaravati. Defeated by Shaka Rudradaman.
Sivasri
Vashishtiputra Sivasri married Rudradaman's daughter. Title: Kshatrapa.
Facts
Last great ruler. Commander: Bhavagopa. Issued Chinnaganjam inscr. Banabhatta's Harshacharita calls him Trisamudradhiswara. Matsya Purana compiled.
Nagarjuna
Patronized Acharya Nagarjuna (born in Vedali, Indian Einstein/2nd Buddha/Tathagata/Indian Martin Luther). Founder of Mahayana. Yajna built the Mahavihara (1500 rooms, 7 floors) at Sriparvata for him.
Literature
Nagarjuna wrote: Suhru Lekhanam (letters to Yajna Sri), Prajnaparamita Sastra, Mula Madhyamaka Sastra, Sunya Saptasati, Lalitavistara, Karandavyuha, Sabhavativyuha. Somadeva's Kathasaritasagaram claims Yajna Sri's son (Yuvaraja) killed Nagarjuna.
Vijaya Sri
Built Vijayapuri near Sriparvata.
Chanda Sri
Issued Kodavali inscription.
Pulamavi IV
Last ruler (Pulamavi III/IV). Issued Myakadoni (Bellary), Holehasan (Dharwad), Adoni (West AP) inscriptions. Overthrown by feudatory Sri Chantamula (Ikshvaku) and fled to Bellary.
Satavahana Society

Admin, Economy & 19 Guilds

  • Administration: Kingdom → Aharas (headed by Amatyas) → Nagaras (headed by Nigama Sabha: 30 members, 6 committees of 5) → Gramas (headed by Gramini/Gulmika).
  • Officials: Mahakaryaka (village dispute solver), Rajukas (judges/judicial), Rajjugahakudu (land surveyor), Gahapatis (caste heads).
  • Economy & Trade: Land tax 1/6th. Artisan tax: Kurukara. Produced "Wootz" steel (evidence in Kondapur, Medak). Foreign traders called Sardhavahulu. Coins: Lead, Potin, Silver (Karshapanas), Gold (Suvarna). 1 Suvarna = 35 Karshapanas.
  • Agricultural Tools: Udaga yantram (ploughing), Ghati yantram (water lifting), Garika yantram (cotton ginning - invented by Rakkasi Lothai under Pulamavi II).
  • 19 Guilds (Srenis): Functioned as banks charging exactly 12% interest. Headed by Setti/Sreshti.
    All 19 Guilds: Kolikalu (weavers), Tilapishtakulu/Telikavaru (oil pressers), Kasakarulu (bronze smiths), Kularulu (potters), Tesakarulu (polishers), Kamarulu (blacksmiths), Halikulu (farmers), Veja (doctors), Udayantrikulu (machine makers), Kanchukulu (palace guards), Kubjalu (elephant caretakers), Pratiharalu (door keepers), Chankukarulu (leather), Gadhikulu (perfumes), Suvarnakarulu (goldsmiths), Vadakulu (carpenters), Selavadakulu (sculptors), Pasakarulu (bamboo/basket), Dhanyakulu (grain merchants).
  • Culture: Oldest Buddhist stupa in AP: Bhattiprolu. Dhanyakatakam called Purvasailam in Buddhist literature. Educational institutes called Ghatikas. Aryadeva wrote Chittashuddi Prakaranam. Acharya Kundakunda wrote Samayasaram.
220 CE – 300 CE

The Ikshvakus (Sri Parvatiya Andhras)

TL;DR Primer

  • Sri Chantamula secured independence; Ehuvala built the 1st South Indian Hindu temples.
  • Virapurushadatta fostered AP's Buddhist Golden Age.
  • Introduced 'Veeragal' (suicide guards) & grandfather-naming traditions.
  • Basics: Capital: Vijayapuri. Emblem: Lion. Mentioned in Bhavishya Purana. Divided kingdom into Rashtras (headed by Mahatalavaras). 5 villages = Grama Panchaka. Started 'Veeragal' and grandfather-naming traditions.
  • Origins: North India (Rapson, Buhler), Karnataka (Vogel, Sten Konow), Tamil Nadu (Gopalachari), Coastal Krishna (Caldwell - most accepted).
  • Guild Leaders: Ulikapramukha (handloom), Pusina Sreni (sweets), Parnika Sreni (betel leaves).
Facts
(Vasishthiputra). Founder. Defeated Pulamavi IV. Devotee of Kartikeya/Virupaksha. Performed Ashvamedha, Agnistoma, Agnihotra, Vajapeya, Mahakrutuvu.
Inscriptions
Issued Rentala, Dachepalli, Kesanapalli.
Titles
Maharaja, Sata Sahasra Halaka (donating agriculture tools/cows), Mahadanadhipati (donating agraharas), Apratihatasankapana.
Buddhism
Only king to embrace Buddhism. "Golden Age of Buddhism in AP". Called "South Indian Ashoka". Built India's 1st Buddhist Univ (Sri Parvata). Issued Alluri Inscr. Fought strictly zero wars.
Temples
First to build Hindu temples in South India. Built Pushpabhadra Narayana, Kartikeya, Nodigiswara (Nandikeswara), and Devi temples at Nagarjunakonda. Commander Elisiri built Kumaraswami temple at Yeleswaram (Nalgonda).
Inscriptions
Issued Nagarjunakonda Inscr (First Sanskrit Inscription in South India).
The End
Last ruler. Built Pushpabhadra Narayana. Defeated by Pallava King Simhavarma, ending the dynasty (Manchikallu Inscription).
Culture
Sivasena (commander of Abhira king Sakasena) built Ashtabhuja Narayana Swamy Temple (1st Vaishnava temple in AP). Harathi temple visited for fertility (offered bangles). Bhavaviveka wrote Tarkajwala, Karataratna, Prajnapradeepa.
300 CE – 624 CE

Contemporaries & Vishnukundins

  • Salankayanas: Capital: Vengi (Pedavegi). Mula Purusha: Vijayadevavarma. Founder: Hastivarma (defeated by Samudragupta; Allahabad Pillar calls him Vaingeyaka). Deity: Chitrarathaswami (Sun). Issued Pedavegi & Guntupalli. Kings: Hastivarma → Nandivarma I (killed by Devavarma) → Devavarma (killed by Achandavarma) → Achandavarma → Vijayanandivarma (Nandivarma II). Conquered Anandas, ended by Vishnukundins (Madhavavarma III).
  • Ananda Gotras: Capital: Kandarapuram (Kandara rulers). Emblem: Vrushabham/Nandi. Victory banner: Golungula (Monkey). Defeated by Salankayanas.
  • Vishnukundins: Language: Sanskrit. Emblem: Lion (Kesari). Admin: Vishayas. Land measured in Nivartanas. Built cave temples (Mogalrajapuram, Undavalli - Anantha Padmanabha Swamy statue).
    • Indravarma: Founder. Title: Priyaputra. Issued Ramatirtham.
    • Order: Indravarma → Madhavavarma I → Govindavarma → Madhavavarma II → Vikramendravarma I & II.
    • Madhavavarma II (Greatest): Defeated Salankayanas/Anandas. Shifted capital: Amarapuram → Indrapalanagaram → Denduluru (Vengi). Titles: Trivaranagara Bhavana Sundari, Janasraya, Trimudradhipati.
    • Inscriptions: Tummalagudem, Chaitanyapuri, Khanapur.
    • Telugu Milestones: First Telugu word 'Nagabu' (Amaravati). First sentence 'Vijaya Rajya Samvatsarambul' (Chikkulla).
624 CE – 9th Century

Eastern Chalukyas: Genesis

Western Chalukya Pulakesin II defeated Vishnukundins, Prithvimula, & Kalingas, gave Vengi to brother Kubja Vishnuvardhana. Admin: Rashtras → Vishayas → Gramas. Tax: 1/6 (Gramakutudu). Emblem: Varaham. Language: Sanskrit. Capitals: Pistapuram → Vengi → Rajahmundry.

Facts
Founder. Titles: Vishamasiddhi, Maharaja (Timmapuram inscr), Makaradhwaja, Kamadeva. Issued Chezerla inscription (>50% Telugu poems). Built Kunti Madhava (Pithapuram).
Culture
Wife Ayyana Mahadevi built Nedumbasadi (Jain rest house in Vijayawada for Kavururi Jains); Kubja donated Mushinikonda village. Hiuen Tsang visited Dhanyakatakam for 2 yrs, studying Tripitakas from Subhuti & Surya. He noted Buddhism's decline, violent locals, and visited Kalinganagara.
Jayasimha I
(Vallabha). Titles: Sarvalokasraya, Sarvasiddhi, Prithvi Vallabha. Shifted capital to Vengi. Founded Sarvasiddhi. Issued Vipparla Inscr. Defeated Boya kings (Pedamaddali Inscr).
Indra Bhattaraka
Ruled 7 days. Title: Tyagathenu. Issued Kondanaguru.
Vishnu II
Title: Pralayaditya. Issued Reyuru. Followed by Mangi Yuvaraja (Samastabhuvanasraya) & Jayasimha II (Niravadya).
Vishnu III
Restored Nedumbasadi. Pallava conflict began.
Vijayaditya I
Dantidurga overthrew Badami Chalukyas to form Rashtrakutas. Feud begins.
Vishnu IV
Supported Govinda I, but defeated by Rashtrakuta Dhruva I (mentioned in Pampa's Vikramarjuna Vijayam). Married his daughter to Dhruva I.
9th – 10th Century CE

Eastern Chalukyas: Golden Age

Battles
Overthrown by Rashtrakuta Govinda III & Bhima Saluki. Fled to Bezawada (named after him). Returned after 12 yrs. Fought 108 battles. (Sataluru inscr by grandson). Title: Narendra Mrigaraja.
Temples
Built 108 Narendra Mrigeswara temples.
Titles
Gunakenalla (beautiful by character), Ranaranga Sudraka, Tripuramartya Maheswara, Parachakrarama, Bhuvanakandarpa, Dakshinapathapati, Prithvi Vallabha, Samadhigata Panchamahashabda.
Wars
Defeated by Amoghavarsha (Vengavalli) then defeated Krishna II (capturing Kiranapura, Achalapura, Tripurapura). Issued Sataluru.
Panduranga
Chief Commander (alongside Kadiyaraju, Vinayadi Sharma, Rajaditya). Conquered Boya Kottas, built Pandurangapuram, built Panduranga Maheswaralayam, issued Addanki Inscription (1st Telugu poetry).
Crisis
Captured by Baddena ("like crocodile in water" - Pampa's Vikramarka Vijayam). Rescued by Mudigonda's Kusumedhudu/Kusumayudha (Title: Vengi Bandhavimukti - Korivi inscr). Battle of Niravadyapura (Nidadavole): son Iramartiganda & Rashtrakuta Gundaya died. Fought 360 battles (Pithapuram inscr by Mallapadeva).
Pancharamas
Built 5 Shiva temples (Kumarabhemeswara, Bhimeswara, Someswara, Ksheeraramalingeswara, Amareswara). Founded Chalukya Bhimavaram. Commander Chattappa built Pardheswara.
Legacy
Donated Attili to singer Challavva (Title: Gandharva Vidya Visarada). Court Poet: Vamana/Bhatta Vamana (wrote Kavyalankara Sutra, title Kavivrushabha).
Kings
Vijayaditya IV: (Kollabiganda, fought Viraja against Kalinga Vajrahasta I). Amma I: (Alt founder of Rajahmundry). Kanthakavi Vijayaditya: (Ruled exactly 15 days). Bhima Raja: (8 months anarchy per Maliyampudi). Yuddhamalla II: (Built Kartikeyalayam, Bezawada inscr, feudatory Nannechoda translated Kumarasambhavam).
10th – 12th Century CE

Eastern Chalukyas: Decline & Chola Merger

Amma II
Titles: Bhattadeva, Madhava Bhattu, Parama Brahmana. Patronized Jains (Kaluchumburru inscr) requested by wife Chamekamba. Kalamukha Shaivism entered.
Danarnava
Killed Amma II with Kakatiya Gundana's help. Issued Magallu inscr. Title: Rajanarayana. Built Mandavya Narayana. Killed by Jata Choda Bhima. (Note: Tailapa II killed Rashtrakuta Kokkaraja II to form Kalyani Chalukyas).
Jata Choda
Telugu Choda. Title: Choda Trinetrudu. Defeated by Rajaraja Chola (Kailasanatha inscr details takeover).
Saktivarma
Crowned by Cholas. Title: Chalukya Chandra (coins in Burma). Vengi became a Chola-Kalyani battlefield. Issued Prabuparru.
Vimaladitya
Titles: Rajamartanda, Mummadi Bhima. Shaiva -> Jain (influenced by Trikalayogi Siddhanti, per Ranastapudi). Built Ramakonda. Married Kundavva (Chola) & Melamma.
Politics
Crowned via Rajendra Chola. Defeated Vijayaditya VII (Battle of Kalidindi). Made vassal by Kalyani Chalukya Someswara, who appointed Narayana Bhattu. Founded Rajahmundry. Title: Kavyagita Pritiparudu.
Nannaya
Adikavi, Vagasasanudu, Vidyadayityudu. Translated 2.5 parvas of Mahabharata. Wrote Andhra Shabda Chintamani in Sanskrit. Wrote Indra Vijayam & Chamundeswari Vilasam in Telugu.
Mallanna
Pavuluri Mallanna (Sukavi, Satkalishudu) translated Ganitasara Sangraham (1st Telugu science book).
Facts
Married Madhurantakini. Founded Kulottunga Cholapuram (Vizag). Jayagondar wrote Kalinga Parani. Handed Vengi to his uncle Vijayaditya VII (Last Ruler).
11th – 12th Century CE

Vengi Feudatories

  • Velanati Cholas/Chodas: Capital: Chandole. Called Andhra "Swabhagini Vishayam" (Relatives' land). Mula purusha: Gonkaraja I. Independence: Gonkaraja II. Battle of Draksharama: Yuvaraja Rajendra II killed Kakatiya Prola II. Poet Manchena (*Keyurabahu Charitam*). Last king Prithviswara killed by Kakatiya Ganapatideva.
  • Kona Haihayas: Capital: Rakuduru. Founder: Kirtiraja. First in AP history to introduce Dual Kingship (Mummadi Bhima II & Satyaraja).
  • Palnati Haihayas: Capital: Gurazala. Founder: Betaraja (Titles: Haihaya Rajya Pratishtakara, Sanivara Siddhi). Anuguraja's sons: Nalagama & Malideva. Ministers: Nagamma (Shaiva, built Virabhadra) & Brahma Naidu (Veera Vaishnava founder, built Chennakeshava).
  • The Palnadu War (1176-1182 CE): Fought between Nalagama (supported by Kakatiya Prataparudra I & Nagamma) and Malideva (supported by Brahma Naidu). Began after a cockfight exile. Nalagama won. Recorded by Srinatha (*Palnati Veera Charitra*) and Mudugu Virabhadra Kavi (*Palnati Virabhagavatam*).
  • Konidena Cholas: King Nannechoda was the 1st "Kaviraja" in Telugu (translated *Kumarasambhavam*).
995 CE – 1157 CE

The Early Kakatiyas (Vassals)

  • Basics: Durjaya Clan (per Bayyaram). Emblem: Varaham. Capitals: Hanumakonda → Orugallu. Language: Sanskrit. Mula Purusha: Kakatiya Gundana (Magallu Inscr) or Venna Bhupati (Bayyaram Inscr).
Beta I
Son of Gundana. Titles: Samantavishtivamsakakatipuradhinatha, Chodakshmapala. Minister Naranayya renovated Yuddhamalla Jain temple (Sanigaram Inscr).
Prola I
Titles: Arigajakesari, Samadhigatapanchamahashabda. Built Kesamudram and Jagatakesarisamudram lakes.
Beta II
Titles: Vikramachakra, Tribhuvanamalla. Issued Kazipet Inscr. Minister Vaija Dandanadhudu (statesman). Built Beteswara. Initiated to Kalamukha Shaivism by Rameswara Pandita.
Durga Raja
Donated Beteswara to Rameswara Pandita.
Facts
Planted victory pillar at Srisailam. Title: Daridraya Bhadravana (for agriculture). Built Swayambhu temple. Minister Betanamatya's wife Mailama built Kadalalalaya Basadi.
Death
Killed in Battle of Draksharama by Velanati Rajendra II.
1158 CE – 1323 CE

The Imperial Kakatiyas

Independence
Declared independence in 1162 CE (Hanumakonda Inscr by Achitendra). Issued Ganapavaram Inscr. Titles: Vinayabhushana, Kavi Bhasha Poshaka. Authored Nitisaram (Sanskrit).
Dev
Built Rudrasamudra lake, 1000 Pillar Temple. Shifted capital to Orugallu. Minister Gangadhara built Prasanna Kesavalayam. Commander Kota Senani captured Dharanikota.
Wars
Supported Nalagama in Palnadu War. Killed by Yadava King Jaitugi (recorded in Hemadri's Chaturvarga Sara Grandham).
Facts
Brother of Rudradeva. Wife: Bayyambika. Children: Ganapatideva, Mailamba (issued Bayyaram Inscr), Kundamamba. Guru: Dhruveswara. Killed by Jaitugi. Ganapati taken hostage.
Facts
Released by Jaitugi's son Singhana (Title: Telungaraya Sthapanacharya) as a buffer state. Ruled 63 yrs. Ganapati's titles: Andhradhishudu, Prithviswara, Krida Vinoda, Chodakataka Churakara, Sakaladesa Pratishtapanacharya. Defeated Velanati Prithviswara.
Recharla Rudra
Commander who saved the kingdom from crisis during Ganapati's capture. Titles: Kakatiya Rajya Bhara Dhaureya, Kakatiya Rajya Samarthudu.
